# Problem 9: Momentum Comparison — Fly vs Tennis Ball

**Given:**
- Fly: $m_1 = 2$ g, $v_1 = 10$ m/s
- Tennis ball: $m_2 = 60$ g, $v_2 = 1$ m/s

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

**Linear momentum:**
$$p = mv$$

**Newton's second law (momentum form):**
$$F = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t}$$

**Units:** kg·m/s

---

## 🧠 The Core Idea

Momentum = mass × velocity. Both factors contribute equally as multipliers.
A 30× mass advantage beats a 10× speed advantage.

---

## Step 1 — Convert to SI (kg)

$$m_{fly}  = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$$
$$m_{ball} = 60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$$

---

## Step 2 — Fly Momentum

$$p_{fly} = 0.002 \times 10 = \boxed{0.020 \text{ kg·m/s}}$$

---

## Step 3 — Ball Momentum

$$p_{ball} = 0.060 \times 1 = \boxed{0.060 \text{ kg·m/s}}$$

---

## Step 4 — Compare

$$\frac{p_{ball}}{p_{fly}} = \frac{0.060}{0.020} = 3$$

$$\boxed{p_{ball} = 3\, p_{fly} \quad \Rightarrow \text{tennis ball wins}}$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

| Object | Mass | Speed | Momentum |
|--------|------|-------|----------|
| Fly | 0.002 kg | 10 m/s | 0.020 kg·m/s |
| Tennis ball | 0.060 kg | 1 m/s | **0.060 kg·m/s** |

$$\boxed{\text{The tennis ball has 3× greater momentum.}}$$

---

## 🔍 Breakeven Speed for the Fly

For the fly to match the ball:

$$v_{fly}^* = \frac{p_{ball}}{m_{fly}} = \frac{0.060}{0.002} = 30 \text{ m/s}$$

The fly would need to travel at 30 m/s (108 km/h) to tie.

---

## 🔍 Why It Matters

$$F = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t}$$

To stop both in 0.1 s:
- Ball needs $0.6$ N of stopping force
- Fly needs $0.2$ N of stopping force

> **Takeaway:** Mass dominates momentum at low speeds.
> A slowly-rolling truck has far more momentum than a fast-moving bicycle.
