# Problem 2: Systems of Equations

**Given:** $2x + 3y = 12$ and $x - y = 1$

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

**Substitution Method:**

$$\text{From equation (2): } x = 1 + y \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{substitute into equation (1)}$$

**Verification Rule:**

$$\text{Plug } (x, y) \text{ back into BOTH original equations — both must hold true}$$

**Geometric Interpretation:**

$$\text{Each equation represents a straight line. The solution } (x, y) \text{ is their intersection point.}$$

---

## Step 1 — Isolate a Variable

Choose the simpler equation. Equation (2) has no coefficients in front of $x$ or $y$, so rearrange it:

$$x - y = 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x = 1 + y$$

> **Why this equation?** Equation (2) is simpler — no multiplication needed to isolate $x$.

---

## Step 2 — Substitute into Equation (1)

Replace every $x$ in equation (1) with $(1 + y)$:

$$2(1 + y) + 3y = 12$$

**Expand the bracket:**

$$2 + 2y + 3y = 12$$

**Combine like terms** ($2y + 3y = 5y$):

$$2 + 5y = 12$$

**Subtract 2 from both sides:**

$$5y = 10$$

**Divide both sides by 5:**

$$\boxed{y = 2}$$

---

## Step 3 — Back-Substitute to Find $x$

Now that $y = 2$ is known, plug it back into the expression from Step 1:

$$x = 1 + y = 1 + 2$$

$$\boxed{x = 3}$$

---

## Step 4 — Verify Both Equations

Always verify by substituting $(x, y) = (3, 2)$ into **both** original equations:

**Check Equation (1):** $2x + 3y = 12$

$$2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12 \quad \checkmark$$

**Check Equation (2):** $x - y = 1$

$$3 - 2 = 1 \quad \checkmark$$

Both equations are satisfied. The solution is confirmed.

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$\boxed{x = 3, \quad y = 2}$$

The two lines intersect at the point $\mathbf{(3,\ 2)}$.
